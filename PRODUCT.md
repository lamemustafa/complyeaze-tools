# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

ComplyEaze visitors, design-system reviewers, engineers, compliance operators,
and potential adopters who need to understand why Sanchika exists before they
trust its packages. They arrive from the public product family, source
repositories, or crawler-readable launch surfaces and need a quick, truthful
answer: what Sanchika proves today, what it refuses to claim, and where it fits
beside Axal, Pack, and Tools.

## Product Purpose

ComplyEaze Tools publishes browser-local compliance utilities and public trust
surfaces. The Sanchika page is the public boundary for the design-system proof
loop: it should make the system feel memorable and carefully engineered while
staying honest about current adoption evidence, static hosting, package
boundaries, and unsupported claims. Success means the page is distinctive,
fast, source-reviewable, and clear about the separation between design
contracts and operational systems.

## Brand Personality

Precise, restless, and trustworthy. The surface should feel like an instrument
for inspecting design proof, not a generic SaaS landing page. It can be
visually ambitious, but the ambition must reinforce evidence, custody, and
reviewability.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic AI landing pages, purple gradients, cream editorial templates,
decorative card grids, oversized metric blocks, vague "beautiful UI" claims,
public-authority endorsement language, production-readiness overclaims, and any copy
that implies Tools uploads, stores, validates, or audits private files.

## Design Principles

- Make proof visible: every memorable visual move should point to a real
  artifact, boundary, or adoption decision.
- Keep custody explicit: Tools is static and browser-local; Sanchika does not
  own clients, credentials, documents, filings, or audit trails.
- Let products differ: Sanchika should unify trust language without flattening
  ComplyEaze, Axal, Pack, and Tools into one visual personality.
- Prefer inspectable craft over decoration: visual complexity must remain
  accessible, performant, and source-reviewable.
- State current truth before future ambition.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA for contrast, focus states, semantic structure, and keyboard
navigation. Motion must be non-essential and respect reduced-motion
preferences. Public copy must remain readable on small screens and avoid
depending on color alone to communicate status.
